Introduction

Welcome to the Apex Arc Lightgun user manual. This guide will help you set up, configure, and use your Apex Arc Lightgun for the best gaming experience.

Getting Started

Setting Up Your Apex Arc Lightgun

  1. Unbox your Apex Arc Lightgun.
  2. Insert batteries or connect the power cable (depending on your model).
  3. Ensure your gaming setup includes a compatible IR bar or sensor.

Connecting to the Configuration Interface

  1. Power on your Apex Arc Lightgun.
  2. On your computer or mobile device, connect to the Wi-Fi network broadcast by the lightgun (default SSID: "ApexArc").
  3. Open a web browser and navigate to the configuration interface (typically at http://192.168.4.1).

Configuration

BLE Device Name

Set a unique Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) name for your lightgun to easily identify it when connecting to your gaming system.

Wi-Fi Settings

Configure the SSID and password for the lightgun's Wi-Fi network. This allows you to connect to the configuration interface.

Trigger Modes

Choose from three trigger modes:

  1. Single Shot: One click per trigger pull.
  2. Burst Mode: Multiple clicks per trigger pull (configurable count and delay).
  3. Full Auto: Continuous clicking while the trigger is held (configurable clicks per second).

Feedback Settings

Adjust haptic feedback settings:

  • Enable or disable feedback
  • Set feedback duration
  • Set feedback delay

Calibration

Follow these steps to calibrate your Apex Arc Lightgun:

  1. Click the "Start Calibration" button on the configuration interface.
  2. Point your lightgun at each corner of your screen as prompted.
  3. Click the trigger when the lightgun is aimed at each calibration point.
  4. After all points are calibrated, your lightgun will be optimized for your screen.

Troubleshooting

  • If the lightgun isn't responding, check the battery level or power connection.
  • Ensure the IR bar or sensor is properly positioned and functioning.
  • If calibration issues persist, try resetting to default settings and recalibrating.

Maintenance and Care

  • Clean the IR sensor on the lightgun regularly with a soft, dry cloth.
  • Store in a cool, dry place when not in use.
  • Remove batteries if storing for an extended period.
